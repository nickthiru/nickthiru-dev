## Business Content Standards

### Structure Framework

#### Business Strategy Posts

1. **Problem Context**: Market gap or business challenge
2. **Traditional Approaches**: What others typically do (and why it fails)
3. **My Approach**: What I tried differently and why
4. **Results & Metrics**: Business outcomes (revenue, growth, efficiency)
5. **Framework**: Repeatable process or mental model
6. **Applicability**: How others can apply this to their situation

#### "Building in Public" Business Posts

1. **Transparency Decision**: What I chose to share and why
2. **Business Impact**: How transparency affected growth/trust
3. **Metrics & Learning**: Specific numbers and key insights
4. **Challenges**: What was difficult about being transparent
5. **Guidelines**: Framework for others to build in public safely

### Writing Style for Business Content

#### Tone & Approach

- **Strategic yet practical**: High-level thinking with actionable steps
- **Data-informed**: Use real metrics and concrete examples
- **Transparent about failures**: Share what didn't work and why
- **Framework-oriented**: Extract repeatable patterns and mental models
- **Audience-aware**: Consider different reader segments and their needs

#### Language Patterns

- **Business terminology**: Use appropriate business language naturally
- **Metrics integration**: Weave numbers and data points throughout
- **Decision frameworks**: Present thinking processes and decision criteria
- **Comparative analysis**: Compare different approaches and trade-offs
- **Actionable insights**: Always include "what you can do with this"
