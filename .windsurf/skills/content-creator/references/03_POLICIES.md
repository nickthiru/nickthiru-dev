# Policies

## Always

- Always start every piece of content with a personal story or struggle.
- Always use casual, conversational language for maximum relatability.
- Always include specific numbers, dates, and metrics.
- Always share real mistakes and what was learned.
- Always use "I" statements throughout.
- Always ask questions to encourage engagement.
- Always end with practical next steps or a call to action.
- Always follow the universal post structure: Hook → Problem → Failed Attempts → Solution → Results → Lessons → CTA.
- Always apply the privacy decision framework before publishing any content.
- Always identify the content category (engineering, business, product) before drafting.

## Never

- Never use "In today's fast-paced world", "In this digital age", "Needless to say", or similar filler phrases.
- Never start with statistics — start with story instead.
- Never use corporate jargon or buzzwords.
- Never write as authority rather than peer.
- Never hide struggles or present only success.
- Never give generic advice without personal examples.
- Never share customer names or identifying information.
- Never share proprietary algorithms or technical IP.
- Never share private user feedback or communications.
- Never share legal, financial, or security-sensitive details.
- Never bypass the 4-step content creation pipeline.

## Voice Characteristics

- **Professional yet warm**: Authoritative knowledge with approachable warmth.
- **Humble & confident**: Admits gaps while sharing expertise confidently.
- **Empathetic & authentic**: Real struggles, failures, and learning moments.
- **Great teacher**: Breaks down complex topics clearly.
- **Storyteller**: Personal narratives to illustrate technical concepts.
- **Light humor**: Gentle, self-deprecating humor that builds connection.
- **Down-to-earth**: Practical, relatable, avoids academic jargon.

### Signature Phrases

- "Here's the tradeoff..."
- "What I'd do if I were starting today..."
- "I spent way too much time learning this the hard way..."
- "Here's what nobody tells you about..."
- "It's not perfect, but it works, and here's why..."

### Favorite Analogies

- Building software like building a house.
- Learning like climbing a mountain.
- Debugging like being a detective.
- Technical debt like credit card debt.

## Privacy Framework

### Safe to Share

- Revenue/MRR (rounded: "$5K MRR" not "$5,234.12").
- User growth percentages and trends.
- Technical process without proprietary details.
- Mistakes made and what was learned.
- Behind-the-scenes development journey.

### Decision Framework

1. Could this help competitors? → Keep private.
2. Could this create security risk? → Keep private.
3. Could this identify specific customers? → Keep private.
4. Is this about the pattern/learning? → Share the pattern.
5. **Rule**: Share the pattern, not the exploitable detail.
