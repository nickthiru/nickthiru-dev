# Daily Content Capture Workflow

## Overview

This workflow implements the proven 15-30 minute daily routine for building in public, turning development work into content systematically.

## When to Use

- Daily routine for consistent content creation
- When building products and sharing progress
- For maintaining audience engagement without burnout
- To create content backlog from development work

## Daily Checklist (15-30 minutes total)

### Morning Routine (5-10 minutes)

**Tasks:**

- [ ] **Reply to comments** on yesterday's posts (LinkedIn, Twitter, blog)
- [ ] **Save 1-3 good posts** from your niche for inspiration
- [ ] **Add 1 bullet** to today's build log (`thiru-ai-labs/build-logs/YYYY-MM-DD.md` at workspace root): what you're trying to ship today

**Why it works:**

- Engagement signals to algorithms that your content is valuable
- Inspiration bank prevents "what should I write about" blocks
- Daily goal creates accountability and focus

### During Development (0 extra minutes - just capture)

**Capture one artifact:**

- Screenshot of new feature
- Short Loom clip (1-2 minutes max)
- Terminal output (sanitized)
- Architecture sketch
- Before/after comparison

**Add 3 bullets to today's build log** (`thiru-ai-labs/build-logs/YYYY-MM-DD.md` at workspace root):

- **Build:** What changed/what you implemented
- **Why:** User value or problem solved
- **Lesson:** What surprised you or what you learned

**Why it works:**

- Turns development work into content automatically
- No extra time needed - just document as you go
- Creates authentic, detailed content later

### End of Day (10-20 minutes)

**Tasks:**

- [ ] **Choose ONE** item from today's build log (from workspace BUILD_LOG.md)
- [ ] **Draft ONE post** (LinkedIn is the default platform)
- [ ] **Post it** or schedule for tomorrow morning
- [ ] **Optional:** Adapt into short X post/thread

**Why it works:**

- One post per day is sustainable and effective
- LinkedIn posts live longer (24-48 hours)
- Repurposing maximizes content reach

## Build Log Templates

### Feature Entry Template

```markdown
**Build:** [what changed]
**Proof:** [screenshot/clip link]
**User Value:** [why it matters to users]
**Tradeoff:** [latency/cost/UX/privacy consideration]
**Next:** [next step or milestone]
```

### Bug/Failure Entry Template

```markdown
**Symptom:** [what broke or went wrong]
**Root Cause:** [what actually caused it]
**Fix:** [what you changed to resolve it]
**Lesson:** [what you'll do differently next time]
```

### User Insight Entry Template

```markdown
**Who:** [role + context of person]
**Problem:** [what they said they needed]
**Quote:** [paraphrased key insight]
**Implication:** [what you'll build/change based on this]
```

## Weekly Process (60-120 minutes)

### Canonical Blog Post

1. **Review week's build logs** - pick strongest theme
2. **Create outline** using blog post template
3. **Write 800-1500 word post** with personal stories
4. **Add code examples** and screenshots
5. **Include lessons learned** and what you'd do differently
6. **End with call to action** (subscribe, waitlist, feedback)

### Multi-Platform Repurposing

1. **LinkedIn Post**: Professional insights (150-300 words)
2. **X Thread**: Key lessons as thread (7-10 tweets)
3. **Newsletter**: Personal update with behind-the-scenes
4. **Schedule posts** for optimal times

## Privacy & Safety Guidelines

### Always Safe to Share

- Revenue metrics (rounded: "$5K MRR" not "$5,234.12")
- User growth percentages and trends
- Technical process without proprietary details
- Learning experiences and mistakes
- Behind-the-scenes development journey

### Never Share Without Permission

- Customer names or identifying information
- Specific revenue figures from enterprise deals
- Proprietary algorithms or technical IP
- Private user feedback or communications
- Legal, financial, or security-sensitive details

### When in Doubt

- Share the pattern, not the exploitable detail
- Share the learning, not the sensitive data
- Ask: "Could this help competitors or create security risks?"

## Common Pitfalls & Solutions

### Pitfall: "I don't have anything to share"

**Solution:** Your daily development work IS the content. Use the workspace build log templates (thiru-ai-labs/build-logs/) to capture it systematically.

### Pitfall: "This takes too much time"

**Solution:** The system is designed for 15-30 minutes total. Capture during development (0 extra time), quick draft at end of day.

### Pitfall: "Nobody is engaging"

**Solution:** Focus on providing value first. Ask questions, respond to every comment, engage with others in your niche.

### Pitfall: "I'm worried about sharing too much"

**Solution:** Follow the privacy guidelines. Share patterns and learnings, not sensitive details.
